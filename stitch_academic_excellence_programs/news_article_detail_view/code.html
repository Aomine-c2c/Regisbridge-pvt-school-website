Annual A-Level Debate Team Secures National Victory at Oxford

calendar_today October 24, 2023
•
person By Sarah Jenkins, Head of English
•
timer 5 min read
home Home chevron_right News chevron_right Debate Team Victory

The annual National Schools Debate Championship concluded yesterday evening with a resounding victory for our Upper Sixth form team. Against a backdrop of intense competition from over fifty schools nationwide, our students demonstrated exceptional rhetorical skill and critical thinking.

It was a tense final round held in the historic chambers of the Oxford Union. Our team, comprised of James Thornton, Priya Patel, and Michael Chang, faced off against the defending champions on the topic of "The Role of Artificial Intelligence in Modern Education." Their arguments were not only well-researched but delivered with the poise and articulation that St. Mary's has come to be known for.

Preparation Meets Opportunity

"We've been preparing for this moment since the start of the academic year," remarked Sarah Jenkins, Head of English and the team's faculty advisor. "The students have sacrificed their lunch breaks and weekends to refine their arguments. This victory is a testament to their dedication and the rigorous intellectual environment we cultivate here at the Academy."

format_quote

"The ability to articulate complex ideas under pressure is perhaps the most valuable skill we will take away from St. Mary's."

— Priya Patel, Team Captain

The victory ensures that St. Mary's will host the regional qualifiers next spring, bringing aspiring debaters from across the county to our campus. This event will align perfectly with the opening of our new Humanities Wing, scheduled for completion in January 2024.

Beyond the trophy, each member of the winning team has been awarded a scholarship for their upcoming university studies, generously sponsored by the Alumni Association.
